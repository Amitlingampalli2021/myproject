About Harvard Business School

For over a century, HBS has shaped the practice of management and the people who practice it.

Our Mission

Harvard Business School's mission is to educate leaders who make a difference in the world. This simple, powerful statement shapes everything we do — from the cases we write to the programs we offer and the research we conduct.

Since 1908, HBS has been at the forefront of management education, pioneering the case method and producing generations of leaders across business, government, and society.

Our History

1908

HBS is Founded

Harvard Business School opens as the first business school to offer an MBA degree, with 59 students and 15 faculty members.

1924

The Case Method

HBS pioneers the case method of teaching, a revolutionary approach that puts students in the role of decision-maker.

1963

Executive Education Expands

HBS establishes comprehensive executive education programs, making world-class business education accessible to working leaders.

2014

HBX Launches

HBS enters online education with HBX (now HBS Online), extending its reach to learners around the globe.

Today

Shaping the Future

HBS continues to innovate in research, education, and social impact — educating leaders for the challenges of tomorrow.
